Introduction

“We all at some point of our lives may be injured or in distress of colossal magnitude. We might as well be close to death calls or make a rare escape. It could be our loved ones or someone acquainted well. In such times, we can react with loss of hope, discouragement, lament and cry or be alert and awake our conscience to regain our strength and act decisively.” Our Team at Nepal Medics and Mountaineers Foundation (NMMF) is certainly bound to respond and save lives when it matters the most while the time is clicking against all odds”


Rescue is not an entitlement of privileged few and Nepalese citizens and visiting tourists deserve better. Collectively this feat can be achieved through hard work and perseverance. We already have a capable team and a roadmap. All we need it to start the engine and shift the gears for the service and care to those in need during critical Medical Evacuation, Search and Rescue, Recovery and repatriation to their native country. Our aim is to remain available 24x7, 365 days a year to avert and mitigate life threatening injury, illness by responding at the earliest like we have done in last 8 years. We strive to rapidly rescue adventure seekers stranded in the remote Himalayan terrain of Nepal.

Nepal is mostly mountainous and mode of transportation long hours away. During Disasters, accidents and in times of immediate medical care need, It takes a long time for the transportation of victims to the nearest Medical Facility. Nepal also hosts more than 700,000 tourist per and the number is expected to rise to over a million in coming years. However, when it matters the most, Nepalese and tourists are at the mercy of few connected agents who can call for helicopters for quick transportation and that too is carried out without the presence of Medics. There is no organization or government body for Central Coordination, through which Medical evacuation, Search and Rescue and Recovery missions can be accomplished. There is no shortages of trained Medics, Mountaineers and Rescue Pilots but such missions are opportunistically carried out haphazardly. There is no doubt that the current way and norms of medical evacuation and Search and Rescue as well as recovery needs to see changes and upgrades through continuous training and development and this team is capable of such breakthroughs. Even the handling of mortal remains should be done with dignity to the deceased.

Therefore, a group of highly trained Medics, Rescue Pilots, Mountaineers/ Rescue Specialists and Rescue Coordinator got together and agreed on a plan to establish Nepal Medics and Mountaineers Foundation (NMMF) to organize and coordinated lifesaving medical evacuations and Search and Rescue in the terrains of Nepal. This team with its years of experience complimented by specific training has what it takes to take charge of this much needed system. We aim to sustain and strengthen the system of Medical Evacuation by land and air as well as the Search and Rescue. Our medic team is capable of accompanying injured victims anywhere in the world.
